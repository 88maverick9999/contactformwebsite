What Makes It Different

Most contact form tools rely on templates — predefined mappings that assume every form has the same fields in the same positions. That works on a handful of sites, but breaks constantly across the variety of real-world contact forms built on different platforms, with custom fields, conditional logic, and varied naming conventions.

Contact Form Blaster's automated form filler takes a different approach: AI semantic field detection. Instead of matching by position or attribute name, the system reads the form the way a human would — understanding the purpose of each field from its label, placeholder, surrounding context, and field type. "Your Message", "Enquiry", "Tell us how we can help", and "Message Body" are all identified correctly as the message field.

This means no setup, no per-site configuration, and no maintenance as websites update their forms. The filler works on the first attempt across the full diversity of websites in your target list.

What the Automated Filler Handles

Every field type, every platform, every protection mechanism.

Standard Text Fields

Name, email, phone, company, address, website, subject, and message — identified and filled correctly regardless of label wording, attribute names, or position in the form.

Dropdowns & Selects

Subject dropdowns, inquiry type selectors, and department routing lists are read and the best-matching option is selected based on your campaign intent.

Checkboxes & Consent

Required consent checkboxes (GDPR, newsletter, terms of service) are detected and checked automatically to allow the form submission to proceed.

CAPTCHA

reCAPTCHA v2, reCAPTCHA v3, and hCaptcha challenges are automatically detected and resolved via integrated solving services before submission proceeds.

Any Platform

Works on WordPress (Contact Form 7, WPForms, Gravity Forms), Squarespace, Wix, Webflow, Shopify, HubSpot embedded forms, and custom-built HTML forms.

Form Discovery

If the contact form isn't on the provided URL, the system crawls internal links (Contact, Get in Touch, Reach Us) to locate the form before attempting submission.
